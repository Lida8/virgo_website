About VIRGO.UA and MMODA

Ukrainian VIrtual Roentgen and Gamma-ray Observatory (VIRGO.UA) is a scientific computing Centre for high-energy astrophysics, aimed to support research in the field of astroparticle physics (computing astroparticle centre) by opening access to observational data from space missions in the X-ray and gamma-ray ranges. VIRGO.UA can be used for student training and education. The center is actively utilized in the teaching process.
https://virgoua.org/en/


VIRGO.UA serves as the point of access in Ukraine for the Multi-Messenger Online Data Analysis (MMODA) platform. The MMODA platform is designed to support multi-messenger astrophysics, which involves analyzing data from various sources (such as gravitational waves, neutrinos, and electromagnetic radiation) to study astrophysical events comprehensively. As a front-end, VIRGO.UA facilitates the local integration of tools, data visualization, and analysis capabilities tailored to Ukrainian researchers or institutions involved in such studies. This collaboration helps streamline access to the MMODA platform's resources, making it easier for scientists to contribute to and benefit from global multi-messenger research efforts.
https://www.astro.unige.ch/mmoda/


The VIRGO.UA has access to the computational resources of the Data Computing Center Of Taras Shevchenko National University of Kyiv.


In particular, a new Kubernetes cluster has been deployed to support the upcoming installation of the MMODA platform. This cluster is connected to uninterruptible power supplies (UPS) and can operate 24/7, remaining nearly unaffected by power outages. The Data Computing Center TSNUK has received a major grant from the National Research Foundation of Ukraine for equipment modernization and the construction of the largest computing cluster in Ukraine. VIRGO.UA is also involved in the implementation of this grant and will have access to computational time on this cluster.

Kubernetes Cluster VIRGO.UA


Kubernetes Cluster consists of 10 nodes:
- 3 control nodes with 4 CPU cores and 8 GB RAM;
- 7 worker nodes for running applications with 8 cores and 16 GB of RAM.
Total:
- 56 Intel(R) Xeon(R) Gold 5220R cores;
- 112 GB RAM for applications.
Additional details:
- Connected Ceph storage with 1 TB capacity.
After deploying new storage, up to 20 TB will be available.
- Host OS for nodes: CentOS 9;
- Kubernetes version: 1.31;
- Deployment tool: kubespray.

VIRGO.UA tasks related to the Multi-Messenger Online Data Analysis (MMODA) platform:


1. Harmonization of Standards for Multi-Messenger Data
1.1 Collaborate on developing and implementing Virtual Observatory (VO) tools and standards for data access and analysis.
1.2 Efficient analysis and modeling of diverse multi-messenger data rely on interoperable formats. Gamma-ray and neutrino communities support the Very-high-energy Open Data Format (VODF), while the Radio Astronomy Interest Group (RIG) focuses on radio data. Developing an open domain-specific and multi-messenger data formats.
1.3 Participate in efforts to harmonize access methods for archives containing public and private data.
2. Provision of Access to Telescope and Detector Data Archives
2.1 Enhance data discovery and access systems to lower barriers for non-expert users.
2.2 Develop modern interfaces for easier identification and access to relevant multi-messenger data.
2.3 Contribute to creating a new ecosystem of FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable) data management.
3. Development of Data Analysis and Modeling Services
3.1 Contribute to integrating Gammapy for multi-messenger analysis of gamma-ray and neutrino data.
3.2 Participate in adapting software and services for joint analysis of multi-messenger datasets.
3.3 Assist in integrating data analysis workflows into the MMODA platform.
4. Integration with MMODA
4.1 Facilitate the aggregation of services for data access, analysis, and modeling within MMODA.
4.2 Support the setup of new services compliant with FAIR principles, leveraging EOSC resources.
4.3 Act as a Ukrainian liaison for MMODA, maintaining a web server that mirrors services for data access, analysis, and modeling.
5. Outreach and Training
5.1 Support training for young researchers and outreach to broader communities, including amateur astronomers and citizen scientists.
5.2 Contribute to integrating MMODA services into educational and outreach activities.
